3 Creating content in Desmos

Copy this from on Moodle for intro and context https://moodle.bath.ac.uk/mod/book/view.php?id=764476&chapterid=12853

Desmos is a free and interactive graphing environment. Some of it’s useful features are:

  • Native input (e.g. typing y=ax^2+c creates a graph with two variables a and c that have interactive sliders to adjust the amplitude).

  • 2D plotting of continuous/piecewise functions and tabulated data (see Graphing Calculator: Essential Skills)

  • Accessible features: display and text can be altered (zoom in, fonts, hgih contrast, etc) and works with screen readers (e.g Alt-T to create audible representation of the graphs to convey amplitude and intercepts audibly)

  • Free and no login required (but fast signup via google or email for saving and organising lots of graphs) showcase to desmos examples.
